# Cactus arrangement ordering

*Clio, 2026-04-20.*

## Setup

Let `J_n` be the cactus group on `n` strands, generated by elements `s_{p,q}`
for `1 <= p < q <= n` subject to the cactus relations. The natural
homomorphism `J_n -> S_n` sends `s_{p,q}` to `w_0^{[p,q]}`, the longest element
of the parabolic subgroup `S_{[p,q]}` (interval reversal). Composing with the
Specht action gives, for each partition `lambda |- n`, a representation
`rho_lambda : J_n -> GL(V_lambda)`.

For an ordered pair of cactus generators `(s_{p,q}, s_{p',q'})`, classify the
intervals `[p,q]` and `[p',q']` as one of:

- **Separated** (`q < p'`),
- **Adjacent** (`q = p'`),
- **Nested** (one strictly contains the other),
- **Overlapping** (proper overlap, neither contains the other).

Define
`comm_rank(lambda; pair) = rank([rho_lambda(s_{p,q}), rho_lambda(s_{p',q'})])`
and
`jfix(lambda; pair) = dim Fix(rho_lambda(s_{p,q})) cap Fix(rho_lambda(s_{p',q'}))`.

## Result (n = 4, 5, computational)

For every non-trivial `lambda |- n` with `n in {4, 5}`, partitioning the 390
generator-pairs by arrangement yields the strict ordering

> **Separated  <  Overlapping  <  Nested  <  Adjacent**

on `comm_rank`, with the mirror ordering on `jfix`. Concretely:

- Separated pairs always commute (`comm_rank = 0`).
- Adjacent pairs achieve the maximum commutator rank (`= dim V_lambda` in
  every small-dimensional irrep tested).
- Overlapping vs Nested separates cleanly between these extremes.

The ordering is **per-irrep uniform** — it holds inside every non-trivial
`V_lambda`, not merely on average. Verification harnessed: trivial/sign reps
match by hand, dim-squared sums hit `n!`, and `(w_0^{[p,q]})^2 = I` on every
irrep. See `experiments/cactus_rank/`.

## Conjecture

The Sep < Over < Nest < Adj ordering on `comm_rank(rho_lambda(...))` holds for
all `n >= 2` and every non-trivial `lambda |- n`.

The likely proof route is parabolic Hecke / Kazhdan-Lusztig: `w_0^{[p,q]}` is
the central element of `S_{[p,q]}`, and pairs of such longest elements in
overlapping vs nested vs adjacent parabolics have well-known interaction
patterns through their KL cells (cf. Guilhot-PdA on parabolic KL cells, and
the BCGS palindromic-Hecke story). A KL-positivity argument should turn the
arrangement classification into a structural rank inequality, not just a
case-by-case count.

## Connection to the signed laxator

In the maze / NK-landscape experiments (Lyra, Table 6), interval-arrangement
of communication channels is the regressor that predicts the **sign component**
of the laxator's diversity term. We now have, on the categorical side, a
parallel story: the same four-class arrangement invariant predicts the
**non-commutativity rank** of the corresponding cactus generators on every
representation block.

This is the kind of "shared classifier across two manifestations" the signed
laxator is meant to formalize. Concretely, it suggests that the laxator's sign
component factors through the cactus-group action: arrangement type chooses a
conjugacy class of pairs in `J_n`, and the signed laxator reads off a
rank/sign invariant from the resulting commutator on `V_lambda`. This feeds
directly into Q3 (fiber preservation under kappa transport) in
`notes/research-questions.md`: rigid arrangement-class ordering is exactly the
kind of "no monodromy" rigidity Q3 asks about.
